Story

The Lost Levels is a side-scrolling platform game similar in style and gameplay to the original 1985 Super Mario Bros., save for an increase in difficulty.[2][3][4][5] As in the original, Mario (or Luigi) ventures to rescue the Princess from Bowser.[4] The player jumps between platforms, avoids enemy and inanimate obstacles, finds hidden secrets (such as warp zones and vertical vines), and collects power-ups like the mushroom (which makes Mario grow), the Fire Flower (which lets Mario throw fireballs), and the Invincibility Star.[2] Unlike the original, there is no two-player mode[6] but at the title screen, the player chooses between the twin plumber characters. Their abilities are differentiated for the first time: Luigi, designed for skilled players, has less ground friction and higher jump height,[2] while Mario is faster.

Characters

You can play Mario and Luigi. The clones of Mario are only available in the GBA version.

Target and other enemies are Shellcreeper, Sidestepper, Fighter Fly, Slipice, Icicle, Fireball, Koopa Troopa and Bowser (Only in GBA version).
